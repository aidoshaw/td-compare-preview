Why teams switch

Why teams leave Twilio for TrueDialog

Twilio built the communications API category, and for developers assembling messaging into a product it is a strong choice. Teams move to TrueDialog when the goal is business texting that works out of the box, not a build.

Pricing that does not spike with volume

Twilio bills per message segment plus passthrough carrier fees, so the invoice climbs as you scale, and dedicated support is a paid add-on. TrueDialog uses flat plans with unlimited users and no carrier markups, so finance can forecast the bill.

A platform, not a coding project

Twilio is an API your developers assemble and maintain. TrueDialog gives marketing and operations a full interface plus the API, so campaigns, two-way inbox, scheduling and reporting are ready on day one. See the enterprise texting platform.

Real, U.S.-based support

Twilio's free tier is email and community; live help costs extra. TrueDialog puts a knowledgeable U.S.-based expert on the phone on every plan, which is the single thing customers mention most.

Texting inside your CRM

TrueDialog integrates natively with Salesforce, HubSpot and Dynamics, so your team texts from the tools they already use, with no middleware to build.

The honest verdict

Which one is right for you

Twilio is a genuinely strong platform. The right choice comes down to how your team works, not marketing spin.

Choose Twilio if

  • You have developers who want raw APIs to build a custom product
  • Messaging is embedded deep inside your own application
  • You are happy to assemble and maintain the stack yourself

Choose TrueDialog if

  • You want a full texting platform, not a coding project
  • You need native Salesforce, HubSpot or Dynamics texting
  • Predictable flat pricing and real U.S.-based support matter
  • You send high volume and deliverability is business-critical
